Building the Giant

At the core of the festivities lies the Ravana effigy. This guide walks you through the process of building it step by step. Use the tabs below to explore each stage, from assembling the robust bamboo frame to adding the finishing decorations. Every phase highlights essential materials and pro tips for an impressive outcome.

1. The Bamboo Skeleton

The frame is essential, offering support and stability. Use sturdy, thick bamboo for the torso and legs, and slimmer pieces for the arms and finer details. Secure all joints tightly with rope or durable wire.

  • Materials: Thick bamboo poles, thin bamboo sticks, strong coir rope, wire.
  • Tip: Build a broad, sturdy base to keep the effigy upright, even in strong winds.
  • Action: Build the torso initially, then connect the legs, arms, and neck. Make sure it‚Äôs all balanced evenly.

2. Creating the Skin

After the frame is prepared, the next step is shaping it and creating a surface for painting. This is usually achieved using paper, cardboard, or stretched cloth.

  • Materials: Old newspapers, colored paper, cardboard, strong adhesive (flour paste), hay/straw.
  • Tip: Stuff the chest and limbs with hay or straw to add bulk, then layer with paper for a fuller, three-dimensional form.
  • Action: Layer sheets of paper with adhesive for durability, letting each one dry fully.

3. Crafting the Ten Heads

Ravana's iconic ten heads stand out as his key trait. Craft each head individually and attach them to the core. Focus on fierce expressions to enhance their intimidating look.

  • Action: Shape the faces, let them set, then coat them in vivid, striking hues. Remember the signature mustache!

4. Final Paint and Fireworks

The last step is painting the effigy to bring it to life, then loading it with fireworks for the big event.

  • Materials: Brightly colored paints, decorative cloths, fireworks, crackers.
  • Tip: Adorn the figure with classic garments and jewelry. Accentuate details using black and gold.
  • Action: Carefully place fireworks inside the body and head cavities, ensuring they are secure. This should ONLY be done by experienced adults.
