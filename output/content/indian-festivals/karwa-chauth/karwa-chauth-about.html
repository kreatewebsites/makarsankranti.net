About Karwa Chauth

Discover the vibrant history, profound symbolism, and unique rituals that define Karwa Chauth as a celebration of love.

Significance & History

Karwa Chauth is a traditional festival tied to various legends, the most popular being the tale of Queen Veeravati, who, deceived by her playful brothers into breaking her fast early, devoted a year of heartfelt prayers to revive her late husband. Yet another story tells of Savitri and Satyavan From the Mahabharata, Savitri's steadfast devotion moved Lord Yama, the god of death, to restore her husband's life.

Traditionally, the festival was marked by women with absent husbands, praying for their safe return. Over time, it transformed into a tribute to marital love and commitment.
