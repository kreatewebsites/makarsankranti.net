A Step-by-Step Ritual Guide

Embrace the cherished customs of Karwa Chauth with this simple guide to honor the rituals with faith and affection.

üíÖ1. The Day Before

Preparations begin with beauty and anticipation. Apply intricate Mehndi (henna) Upon your hands and feet, a mark of love and fortune. Prepare all essentials for the Sargi and Puja.

üåÖ2. The Sargi

Before sunrise, partake in the SargiA pre-dawn meal from your mother-in-law fuels the day's fast. After eating, the Nirjala Vrat (waterless fast) commences.

üôè3. The Fast (Vrat)

Observe a strict Nirjala Vrat From dawn to dusk, fasting without food or water, the day fills with prayers, bhajans, and evening ritual prep.

üìñ4. Evening Puja & Katha

In the evening, adorn elegant bridal wear. Women unite to conduct group Puja, hearing the Karwa Chauth Katha (story)Puja thalis, adorned with decorations, are circulated in a circle during prayers.

üåï5. Sighting the Moon

Once the moon is visible, view its reflection through a sieve (chhalni)...then gaze at your husband's face. Present water (arghya) to the moon, praying for his long life.

üéâ6. Breaking the Fast

Following the moonlit prayer, the husband gives his wife her first sip of water and a taste of food, ending her fast. The evening wraps up with a festive family meal and cheerful celebrations.

Puja Thali Essentials

Prepare your Puja thali with these holy essentials for the evening ritual.

A decorated Karwa
A diya (lamp)
A sieve (chhalni)
Incense sticks
Sweets (mathri)
Fruits & flowers
Roli & akshat
A glass of water
