Diwali: The Festival of Lights

Diwali, also known as Deepavali, is one of the most important Hindu festivals celebrated across the globe. It is a five-day festival that marks the victory of light over darkness, good over evil, and knowledge over ignorance. The festival is celebrated with great enthusiasm and joy, with millions of lamps illuminating homes, temples, and public spaces.

The festival commemorates the return of Lord Rama, his wife Sita, and his loyal devotee Hanuman to Ayodhya after 14 years of exile. Lord Rama is considered the seventh avatar of Lord Vishnu and is revered for his courage, virtue, and devotion to dharma. Sita, his wife, is an embodiment of purity and virtue, while Hanuman, the monkey god, is celebrated for his unwavering devotion and service to Lord Rama.

The spirit of 'Ram Rajya', the reign of Lord Rama, is often invoked during Diwali. Ram Rajya is considered the ideal state of governance, characterized by peace, prosperity, righteousness, and justice. It is a time when people are encouraged to uphold the values of truth, morality, and righteousness in their lives.
